1. Introduction

GREVO Multipurpose Cooperative Society ("GREVO", "we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and personal data of our members, website visitors, partners, and all individuals who interact with our services. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or engage with our agricultural services.

We are a registered cooperative headquartered in Luansobe, Mufulira, Copperbelt Province, Zambia. Our operations are governed by Zambian law, and we also align our data practices with internationally recognised standards including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) principles, particularly as we engage with international partners and funders.

By using our website or submitting your information to us, you consent to the practices described in this policy. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use our services or provide us with your personal information.

Summary: We collect only what we need, use it only for legitimate purposes, never sell your data, and give you full control over what you share with us.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use the personal data we collect for the following purposes:

  • Member Management: Processing membership applications, issuing GREVO farmer IDs, managing member records and communications.
  • Service Delivery: Providing access to our IoT sensor network, drone services, AI forecasting tools (YieldMind), and training programmes.
  • Agricultural Support: Analysing farm data to provide personalised crop recommendations, yield forecasting, and pest/disease alerts.
  • Communications: Responding to enquiries, sending programme updates, news, and agricultural advisories to members who have opted in.
  • Research & Impact Reporting: Aggregating anonymised farm data to measure cooperative impact, prepare reports for funders, and conduct agricultural research.
  • Legal Compliance: Maintaining records as required under Zambian cooperative law and responding to lawful government requests.
  • Website Improvement: Analysing website usage patterns to improve our digital services and user experience.
  • Grant Applications: Using anonymised aggregate data to support applications to funders like timbuktoo, AGRA, and AfDB.

We will never use your data for automated decision-making that produces significant legal effects on you without your explicit consent and the ability to seek human review.

4. Data Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data. We may share your data only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service Partners: Third-party technology providers (e.g., IoT platform vendors, drone operators, cloud hosting providers) who process data on our behalf under strict data processing agreements.
  • Cooperative Partners: Research institutions such as Mukuba University for agricultural studies, only with your knowledge and in anonymised or aggregated form where possible.
  • Funders & Auditors: Programme funders (AGRA, ZATP II, etc.) and auditors who require impact evidence — only aggregated, anonymised data is shared unless your individual consent is obtained.
  • Regulatory Authorities: Zambian government bodies including the Registrar of Societies or the Patents and Companies Registration Agency (PACRA) as required by law.
  • Legal Requirements: When disclosure is necessary to comply with a legal obligation, court order, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of GREVO, our members, or the public.

We will never sell your personal data to advertisers, data brokers, or any commercial third parties.

Where we share data with international partners (e.g., pan-African networks or UN bodies), we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place including standard contractual clauses or equivalent protections.

5. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to enhance your browsing experience. We use the following types:

  • Strictly Necessary Cookies: Essential for the website to function. These cannot be disabled (e.g., session management, form security tokens).
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our website (e.g., page views, time on page). We use privacy-respecting analytics and anonymise IP addresses where possible.
  • Functional Cookies: Remember your preferences such as language or region settings.

We do not use advertising/tracking cookies, retargeting pixels, or social media tracking widgets that report your behaviour to third parties without consent.

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling strictly necessary cookies may impair website functionality. For more details, see our Data Policy.

6. Your Rights

Under the Zambia Data Protection Act and GDPR principles, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your personal data ("right to be forgotten") where there is no legitimate reason to continue processing it.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Request that we limit how we use your data while a dispute is resolved.
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your personal data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to Object: Object to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Where processing is based on consent, withdraw it at any time without affecting prior lawful processing.
  • Right to Complain: Lodge a complaint with the relevant Zambian data protection authority.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at grevomultipurpose@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days.

7. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, loss, destruction, or alteration. Our security measures include:

  • HTTPS encryption for all data transmitted via our website
  • Access controls limiting who within GREVO can access member data
  • Regular review of our data handling practices and systems
  • Staff training on data protection and confidentiality obligations
  • Secure disposal of physical records containing personal data

Despite these measures, no data transmission over the internet or storage system can be guaranteed to be 100% secure. If you suspect a data breach affecting your information, please contact us immediately.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected or as required by law:

  • Member records: Retained for the duration of membership plus 7 years after termination, as required for cooperative accounting and legal purposes.
  • Enquiry/contact data: Retained for 2 years unless a continuing relationship develops.
  • Farm & IoT data: Aggregated and anonymised for long-term agricultural research; identifiable records retained for 5 years.
  • Website analytics: Retained for 13 months in anonymised form.
  • Financial transaction references: Retained for 7 years in compliance with Zambian tax and audit requirements.

When data is no longer needed, we securely delete or anonymise it.

10. Policy Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, legal requirements, or services. When we make material changes, we will:

  • Update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page
  • Notify registered members via email or WhatsApp where the changes are significant
  • Post a notice on our website homepage for at least 30 days

We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Continued use of our website or services after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.

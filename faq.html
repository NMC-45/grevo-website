Got Questions? We've Got Answers.

Everything you need to know about GREVO — from membership to technology

18 Questions Answered Below
🌾 About GREVO
GREVO Multipurpose Cooperative Society is Zambia's leading youth-led AgriTech cooperative, headquartered in Luansobe, Mufulira, Copperbelt Province. Founded in 2020 with 48 founding members, GREVO has grown to over 1,800 active members across 12 districts. We combine IoT sensor technology, AI yield forecasting, drone crop monitoring, blockchain traceability, and traditional cooperative values to empower smallholder farmers with dignified livelihoods and access to pan-African markets.
GREVO is headquartered in Luansobe Area, Mufulira District, Copperbelt Province, Zambia. Our membership currently spans 12 districts, with the highest concentration in the Copperbelt (Mufulira, Kitwe, Ndola, Chingola). We are actively expanding across all 10 provinces of Zambia and have established a strategic roadmap for pan-African expansion to neighbouring countries including Malawi, Zimbabwe, and DRC.
Yes. GREVO Multipurpose Cooperative Society is officially registered under the Zambia Cooperative Societies Act with the Registrar of Societies. Our registration provides legal standing to hold assets, enter contracts, apply for grants, and operate as a formal cooperative entity. Copies of our registration certificate are available on request via email: grevomultipurpose@gmail.com.
👥 Membership
Joining is simple. Complete our online membership application, pay the ZMW 500 once-off joining fee, attend an orientation session, and receive your GREVO Farmer ID. You can also apply in person at our Luansobe office or contact us on WhatsApp at +260 761 441 893 to be guided through the process by a membership coordinator.
The once-off registration fee is ZMW 500. This covers your GREVO Farmer ID card, orientation session materials, and initial platform onboarding. Annual membership fees (if any) are determined by the membership at the Annual General Meeting (AGM). We ensure that no farmer is denied membership purely on financial grounds — please contact us if circumstances are difficult.
Absolutely yes. GREVO actively encourages and prioritises the inclusion of women at every level — from general membership to leadership positions. Over 40% of our current members are women. We have specific training tracks, mentorship programmes, and leadership development pathways designed for women farmers. GREVO's co-founder and Training Coordinator Kezia Mwale leads our gender inclusion programme, which aligns with SDG 5 (Gender Equality).
GREVO operates on the cooperative principle of equitable surplus distribution. At the end of each financial year, after operational costs and reserve requirements are met, any surplus is distributed to members proportional to their participation and production contribution. Specific distribution ratios are approved at the Annual General Meeting. Since 2021, GREVO members have collectively received over ZMW 2.4 million in income and surplus distributions.
📡 Technology
No. While a smartphone greatly enhances access to the GREVO mobile app, IoT data dashboards, and AI recommendations, it is not a requirement for membership. Our field coordinators provide personalised support for members without smartphones, delivering data reports in person or via SMS. We also conduct regular in-person group sessions at community hubs where all members can access the platform together.
FarmPulse IoT sensors are installed in your field by GREVO-trained technicians. The sensors continuously measure soil moisture, temperature, pH, and nutrient levels at depth, transmitting data via cellular networks to our cloud platform. You receive alerts on your phone if critical thresholds are crossed (e.g., soil too dry, frost risk). Drone surveys add aerial imagery to complete the picture. All data feeds into the YieldMind AI for personalised recommendations.
FarmPulse is GREVO's proprietary IoT sensor network that monitors agricultural conditions across all member farms in real time. As of 2025, FarmPulse covers over 4,200 acres across 12 districts. The network provides an integrated picture of soil health, weather patterns, and crop stress across the cooperative — enabling both individual farm recommendations and collective early warning systems for pests and disease outbreaks.
GREVO's AeroCrop drone fleet (6 units active as of 2025) performs regular aerial surveys of member farms. Drones capture multispectral imagery that reveals crop stress, pest pressure, weed invasion, waterlogging, and irrigation gaps — often weeks before these are visible to the naked eye. This early detection allows members to take corrective action in time, significantly reducing crop losses. Drone surveys are scheduled seasonally for all members.
YieldMind is GREVO's artificial intelligence system trained on historical agricultural data, satellite imagery, soil science, and regional weather patterns. It analyses each member's farm data to provide personalised recommendations: optimal planting dates, fertiliser schedules, irrigation timing, pest management interventions, and expected yield ranges. YieldMind achieves 92% forecasting accuracy on maize yield projections across Copperbelt member farms, based on 2024 validation data.
Yes. Blockchain traceability is included as part of standard GREVO membership for produce sold through cooperative market channels. GREVO uses a lightweight blockchain ledger to record the chain of custody for member produce — from planting to processing to delivery. This allows premium buyers, exporters, and food companies to verify origin and quality, enabling members to command premium prices. There is no additional fee for this service.
🌍 Languages, Access & Programmes
GREVO delivers all its training, orientation, and farmer support in both English and Bemba. Bemba is the primary language of the Copperbelt, and we are committed to ensuring no language barrier prevents any farmer from fully benefiting from our programmes. We are also exploring support for Nyanja, Tonga, and other widely spoken Zambian languages as we expand to new provinces.
GREVO currently supports maize (white maize), soybeans, groundnuts, sunflower, and various vegetables (tomatoes, rape, cabbage). Our broiler chicken programme is also well-established. IoT and AI support is most advanced for maize and soybeans. We are expanding support to sweet potato, cowpeas, and cassava in the 2026 season, driven by member demand and nutritional diversity goals.
Currently, GREVO does not operate a formal loan programme, but we facilitate access to input financing through our ZATP II programme partnerships and connect members with approved microfinance institutions. Our cooperative model also enables bulk purchasing of seeds, fertiliser, and chemicals at subsidised group rates — which significantly reduces upfront costs for members. A formal cooperative savings and credit scheme is under development for 2026–2027.
In March 2026, GREVO submitted a competitive application to the timbuktoo GreenTech Hub — a pan-African accelerator for technology-driven climate and agricultural solutions. This programme, part of UNDP's timbuktoo initiative, would provide GREVO with mentorship, funding, pan-African market connections, and scale-up support. Our application highlights our IoT network, AI platform, and impact on over 1,800 Zambian farmers. Results are expected in mid-2026.

2. Legal Basis for Processing

Under the Zambia Data Protection Act 2021 and in alignment with GDPR principles, every processing activity must have a valid legal basis. GREVO relies on the following:

  • Contractual Necessity: Processing required to deliver cooperative membership services and honour our obligations to members — including IoT sensor access, AI forecasting, drone services, and training programmes.
  • Legitimate Interests: Processing necessary for GREVO's legitimate operational interests including internal reporting, impact measurement, service improvement, and fraud prevention — balanced against your rights and interests.
  • Legal Obligation: Processing required to comply with Zambian cooperative law, tax regulations, the Registrar of Societies, or court orders.
  • Consent: Processing for activities where you have actively opted in — including newsletter subscriptions, sharing testimonials, and optional research participation. Consent can be withdrawn at any time.
  • Public Interest / Scientific Research: Aggregated and anonymised agricultural data used for research that benefits food security and smallholder farmers across Zambia.

We record our legal basis for each processing activity and make this information available on request.

4. Security Measures

GREVO takes the security of your personal data seriously. We implement a layered approach to data security:

  • Encryption in Transit: All data transmitted between your browser and our website is encrypted using TLS/HTTPS. IoT sensor data is transmitted via encrypted protocols.
  • Access Controls: Personal data is accessible only to authorised GREVO staff and volunteers on a strict need-to-know basis. All access is logged.
  • Cloud Security: Cloud-hosted data is stored with reputable providers that maintain ISO 27001 or equivalent certifications and support data residency preferences.
  • Staff Training: All GREVO staff who handle personal data receive training on data protection, confidentiality, and the Zambia Data Protection Act.
  • Incident Response: We have a data breach response protocol. In the event of a breach affecting your rights, we will notify you and the relevant authority within 72 hours of discovery.
  • Physical Security: Paper records containing personal data are stored in locked facilities. Sensitive waste is shredded prior to disposal.
  • Vendor Management: All third-party service providers who handle personal data on our behalf must agree to data processing agreements with security and confidentiality obligations.

5. International Data Transfers

GREVO primarily operates within Zambia. However, some of our service providers and partners (including cloud hosting, email services, and analytical tools) may process data in other countries. Where we transfer data outside Zambia, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or equivalent contractual protections with international data processors
  • Data Processing Agreements with clear obligations on security, purpose limitation, and sub-processing restrictions
  • We prefer providers with African data centres where available and practical
  • For data shared with international funders (e.g., AGRA, AfDB, UN bodies), we share only aggregated anonymised data unless you have separately consented to identified data sharing

You may request information about the specific international transfers that may apply to your data by contacting our Data Officer.

6. Your Rights

Under the Zambia Data Protection Act 2021 and GDPR principles, you hold the following rights. We honour all of them without requiring you to give reasons:

👁️

Access

Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you. We will provide it within 30 days, free of charge.

✏️

Rectification

Ask us to correct any inaccurate or incomplete data we hold about you.

🗑️

Erasure

Request deletion of your data where there is no overriding legitimate reason to retain it.

⏸️

Restriction

Ask us to pause processing of your data while we resolve a dispute or verify accuracy.

📦

Portability

Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format (CSV/JSON) to transfer to another service.

🚫

Object

Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing at any time.

↩️

Withdraw Consent

Where processing relies on consent, withdraw it at any time without affecting prior processing.

⚖️

Complaint

Lodge a complaint with the Zambia Information & Communications Technology Authority (ZICTA) or equivalent regulator.

To exercise any right, contact our Data Officer at grevomultipurpose@gmail.com with the subject line "Data Rights Request". We will acknowledge within 5 working days and respond fully within 30 days.

7. Cookies Policy

We use cookies and similar technologies on this website. A cookie is a small text file placed on your device when you visit a website. Here is a detailed breakdown of the cookies we use:

Essential
Strictly Necessary Cookies

Required for the website to function. Include session management and form security tokens. Cannot be disabled. Expire at end of browser session or after 24 hours.

Analytics
Analytics Cookies

Help us understand how visitors navigate our site (page views, time on page, referral sources). IP addresses are anonymised. Data is aggregated and not used to identify individuals. Retained for 13 months. You can opt out.

Functional
Functional Cookies

Remember your preferences such as language, previously viewed content, or form data to improve your experience. Retained for up to 12 months.

We do not use advertising, tracking, or social media cookies. We do not share cookie data with advertisers. You can manage or delete cookies through your browser settings at any time. Note that disabling essential cookies may affect website functionality.
